## 🎛 Featured Projects

### Tommy

A transparent, touch-sensitive overdrive plugin inspired by the classic Timmy pedal.

Tommy is a circuit-modelled audio plugin built for guitarists who want natural breakup and dynamic response rather than generic distortion. It uses Wave Digital Filter circuit modelling rather than capture or profiling techniques.

🔗 https://github.com/tehguitarist/Tommy

---

### Monarch of Tone

A dual-channel boutique overdrive inspired by Bluesbreaker-style circuits also known as the King of Tone. This one doesn't have a 6 year waiting list either!

Monarch of Tone expands on the single-pedal workflow by providing two independently voiced drive stages that can be stacked for everything from subtle edge-of-breakup tones to saturated blues-rock drive. Like Tommy, it's built using component-level circuit modelling and is designed to behave like the hardware rather than simply emulate its sound. Even had one person say it is really close to their real pedal both in frequency response, grain of distortion, and how it cleans up. Can't ask for higher praise than that.

🔗 https://github.com/tehguitarist/Monarch-of-Tone

---

### BPM2Time

A simple utility for converting musical tempo into practical delay and timing values.

Whether you're dialing in analogue delays, synchronising hardware, or calculating note lengths for production, BPM2Time provides quick conversions between BPM and common musical note divisions without needing a calculator.

🔗 https://github.com/tehguitarist/BPM2Time

---

### Note2Freq

A musician-friendly note frequency reference.

A lightweight tool that shows you what musical notes their corresponding frequencies, making it useful for synthesis, DSP development, tuning experiments and anyone working with pitch mathematically.

🔗 https://github.com/tehguitarist/Note2Freq
